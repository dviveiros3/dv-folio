# Jules Operating Brief For Trinity

## Read This First
Jules is event-driven and may only see the issue or PR context. Do not assume the agent has read the whole repo. Every Trinity task should treat this brief as the shared operating context.

Trinity is not a generic AI agency, prompt library, or pile of generated product ideas. Trinity is Daniel Viveiros' applied AI lab and product engine. The goal is to build visible proof that Daniel can identify valuable workflows, package AI into usable systems, and turn those systems into reputation, distribution, pipeline, and eventually product revenue.

The work should help Trinity become:

1. **Image Layer:** Daniel is perceived as an AI application leader and implementation strategist.
2. **Product Layer:** Trinity contains sellable AI skills, workflows, kits, audits, and implementation systems.
3. **Proof Layer:** Trinity shows artifacts, examples, diagnostics, lab notes, and honest experiments.

## Current Strategic Center
The strongest wedge is the **Trinity Growth Kit** and its **Distribution OS**.

For a solopreneur/founder, distribution is the constraint. The Growth Kit is not generic marketing automation. It is an AI-assisted operating system for turning expertise into:

- point of view;
- proof artifacts;
- qualified conversations;
- productized offers;
- conversion learning;
- repeatable distribution.

## Product Language
Prefer concrete product nouns:

- Audit
- Diagnostic
- Workflow
- Skill
- Kit
- Implementation
- Lab Exhibit
- Proof Artifact
- Buyer Intake
- Operating Cadence
- Distribution Loop

Avoid vague commodity language:

- AI transformation
- unlock your potential
- 10x growth
- fully automated revenue
- AI-powered everything
- game-changing
- revolutionary
- effortless

## Proof Discipline
Trinity can be ambitious, but it cannot fake traction.

Never invent or imply:

- clients;
- case studies;
- revenue generated by Trinity;
- lead counts;
- conversion rates;
- PE/VC customer usage;
- production deployments;
- guaranteed savings;
- guaranteed pipeline;
- guaranteed valuation impact.

Allowed framing:

- "designed to";
- "intended to";
- "diagnostic";
- "roadmap";
- "sample / fictional";
- "hypothesis";
- "target outcome";
- "manual-first workflow";
- "requires human review";

If using example metrics, clearly label them fictional or simulated.

## Quality Bar
Good Trinity work should feel:

- commercially useful;
- specific enough for a buyer to inspect;
- premium without being fluffy;
- founder/operator oriented;
- proof-safe;
- integrated with existing files;
- small enough to review.

Bad Trinity work:

- adds many shallow files;
- creates generic SaaS pages;
- rewrites unrelated copy;
- changes package files without being asked;
- adds dependencies;
- invents proof;
- creates fake links or fake domains;
- repeats generic AI marketing language.

## Merge-Safe Scope Rules
For most Jules tasks:

- Keep PRs narrow.
- Do not touch `package.json`, lockfiles, or dependency setup unless the issue explicitly asks for repo hygiene.
- Do not restructure raw `trinity/` folders unless the issue explicitly asks.
- Do not edit unrelated app routes.
- Do not add large new dependencies.
- Prefer Markdown artifacts for product strategy and proof examples.
- If editing `app/trinity/page.tsx`, keep changes focused and verify the route still builds.

## Trinity Storefront Direction
The `/trinity` page should make a visitor understand:

1. Trinity is an applied AI lab.
2. The Growth Kit is about founder distribution mastery.
3. The Growth Audit is the entry offer.
4. The catalog has other kits, but Growth is the wedge.
5. Custom implementation exists for serious buyers.

The page should not pretend there is a checkout, customer portal, or proven SaaS product unless those features exist.

## Growth Audit Direction
The AI Growth Audit is the first monetizable entry offer. It should be presented as a diagnostic and roadmap, not a promise of leads or revenue.

It should inspect:

- founder point of view;
- proof inventory;
- current channels;
- buyer conversations;
- offer ladder;
- AI workflow opportunities;
- 30/60/90-day distribution roadmap.

It should produce artifacts a buyer can understand: intake checklist, diagnostic report, roadmap, workflow design, and next implementation options.

## Recommended PR Structure
Every Jules PR should include:

- what changed;
- why it matters to Trinity;
- files changed;
- proof-safety notes;
- verification performed;
- what it intentionally did not touch.

## Before Opening A PR
Ask these checks:

- Is this helping Trinity become more visible, sellable, or credible?
- Is the work specific enough to inspect?
- Did I avoid invented proof?
- Did I avoid broad unrelated edits?
- Did I link or update the relevant source docs?
- Would Codex be able to merge this without cleanup?

If the answer is no, narrow the PR.
