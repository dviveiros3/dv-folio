# AI Growth Audit: Paid Beta Test Plan

## Mission
To test the market viability of the AI Growth Audit as a high-conviction entry wedge. This plan focuses on identifying 3-5 qualified beta conversations with buyers who may be willing to pay for a diagnostic and roadmap before Trinity scales into full kit implementations.

## 1. Beta Hypothesis
We are testing whether technical founders and subject matter experts (SMEs) perceive enough value in a **diagnostic-only** engagement to pay for it.

*   **Primary Question:** Will a buyer pay for a blueprint (The Audit) without a guarantee of implementation or leads?
*   **Secondary Question:** Does the [Buyer Intake Checklist](../catalog/growth-kit/growth-audit-intake.md) provide enough signal to produce a high-utility roadmap without exhaustive manual consulting?

## 2. Buyer Qualification Criteria

### Ideal Fit
- **Profile:** Technical founders or SMEs with deep domain expertise.
- **Proof:** Has at least 3-5 existing "proof assets" (case studies, results, high-signal content).
- **Bottleneck:** Posting "random acts of content" or manual outreach that feels like a treadmill.
- **Mindset:** Values systems over "magic prompts" and understands AI as leverage for judgment.

### Not a Fit
- **The "Magic Button" Seeker:** Expects 1,000 leads or automated revenue overnight.
- **The Zero-Proof Founder:** No product, no expertise, and no historical results.
- **The Commodity Buyer:** Looking for the cheapest possible prompt-generation service.
- **The "Anti-AI" Purist:** Unwilling to experiment with AI-assisted extraction and atomization.

## 3. Offer Shape (Beta Version)

### Deliverables (1-Week Timeline)
1.  **POV & Thesis Audit:** Mapping the founder's expertise to a defensible market point of view.
2.  **Distribution Loop Diagnostic:** Visual mapping of the 5 loops (POV, Proof, Conversations, Offers, Conversion) to identify the primary leak.
3.  **Custom AI Skill Design:** 2 architectural specs for AI workflows (e.g., Insight Extractor, Outbound Atomizer).
4.  **90-Day Implementation Roadmap:** A prioritized sequence for deploying Growth Kit modules.

### Buyer Inputs
- Completion of the [Buyer Intake Checklist](../catalog/growth-kit/growth-audit-intake.md).
- One 15-minute voice/video brief.
- Access to existing proof assets.

### Next-Step Implementation Paths
- **Path A:** Internal build using the Audit roadmap and specs.
- **Path B:** Full Trinity Growth Kit implementation.
- **Path C:** Custom implementation for complex enterprise/PE workflows.

## 5. Beta Outreach Language

### Option 1: The "Architectural Wedge" (LinkedIn/DM)
> "Hey [Name], I’m pressure-testing an AI Growth Audit I’m hardening at Trinity. It’s a 1-week diagnostic designed to move founders from random content into a repeatable Distribution OS. Given your depth in [Industry], I’d value your read on whether the roadmap would be useful for someone in your position. Open to being an early reviewer?"

### Option 2: The "System vs. Slop" (Direct Message)
> "[Name], been seeing a lot of AI content slop lately. I’m testing a paid beta for a Growth Audit that does the opposite: it uses AI to extract specific taste and proof into a system. I’m looking for operator feedback on the diagnostic output. If you’re open to a blueprint-first approach to distribution, I can send over the details."

### Option 3: The "Bottleneck" Inquiry
> "Enjoyed your recent post on [Topic]. It’s clear you have the Proof, but I’m curious if you feel like your 'Conversation Loop' is keeping up. I’m running a small beta group for a diagnostic that maps these leaks. No outcome guarantees—just an engineering-grade roadmap of your distribution engine. Interested?"

## 6. Objection Handling
Refer to the [AI Growth Audit: Objection Bank](./growth-audit-objection-bank.md) for responses to:
- "Is this just prompts?"
- "Can you guarantee leads?"
- "I don't have time for a long project."
- "I'm worried about AI slop."

## 7. Proof Capture Plan
For every beta lead or participant, log the following in the [Proof Capture Log](./proof-capture-log.md):
- **Exact Objection Phrasing:** How do they describe their skepticism?
- **Value Signal:** Which deliverable (POV, Loop Map, Skill Design, or Roadmap) resonated most?
- **Price Resistance:** At what point did the price hypothesis trigger a "no"?
- **Intake Friction:** Did the checklist feel like "work" or "clarity"?

## 8. No-Go Guardrails (Proof-Safe Compliance)
- **No Guarantees:** Do not promise leads, revenue, or specific conversion percentages.
- **No Fake Scarcity:** Do not use "only 2 hours left" or other high-pressure tactics.
- **No Fake Proof:** Do not imply we have 100+ audit graduates if we are in beta.
- **No Commodity Language:** Avoid "revolutionary," "game-changing," or "fully automated."
- **Qualified Verbs Only:** Use "designed to," "intended to," "aims to," and "experimental."
