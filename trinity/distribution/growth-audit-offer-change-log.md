# AI Growth Audit: Offer-Change Log & Improvement Rules

## Purpose
The Growth Audit offer is the primary monetizable wedge of the Trinity [Distribution OS](../catalog/growth-kit/distribution-os.md). This log ensures that every update to the offer is driven by documented market signal rather than speculation. It operationalizes the "Offer Intelligence Loop" by turning buyer language, objections, and conversation quality into concrete asset improvements.

## Confidence Labels
Before making a change, categorize the signal using these levels:

| Label | Meaning | Threshold for Change |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Verified** | Confirmed by payment, signed agreement, approved screenshot, or attributable public source. | High: Eligible for core copy updates if the claim stays evidence-backed. |
| **Observed** | Directly witnessed in a recorded call, email, or DM thread. | Medium: Update to deliverables or intake. |
| **Anecdotal** | Reported qualitatively or from a single unverified conversation. | Low: Add to "test next" but don't change core copy. |
| **Simulated** | Fictional or sample data used for demonstration. | N/A: Never use to justify a public claim. |
| **Hypothesis** | An assumption about buyer behavior being tested. | N/A: Used only to design new tests. |

## Improvement Rules: When to Update

### 1. Update Copy (Headline / Subheadline)
- **Rule:** When 3+ qualified prospects use the same noun/verb to describe their pain.
- **Rule:** When a "Hypothesis" headline fails to generate any DMs over 2 weeks of publishing.

### 2. Update Intake Requirements
- **Rule:** When a discovery call is wasted because the founder lacks a specific asset (e.g., no existing proof).
- **Rule:** When the "Time to Value" objection is tied to a complex intake form.

### 3. Update Deliverables
- **Rule:** When a buyer asks "What about [X]?" and [X] is a repeatable workflow we can document.
- **Rule:** When a simulated sample artifact receives more interest than the actual current deliverables.

### 4. Update Pricing Language
- **Rule:** When a prospect accepts a price point without any friction (indicates room for implementation upsell).
- **Rule:** When "Cost vs ROI" objections repeat despite showing the 1-week timeline.

### 5. Update Publishing CTAs or Implementation Upsell
- **Rule:** When a specific Distribution OS loop (e.g., "Proof") generates more qualified DMs than the others.
- **Rule:** When the transition from POV to "Audit" feels too abrupt in conversation.
- **Rule:** When a buyer asks "Who builds this?" more than twice in one week, update the "Implementation Path" in [Sales Page](../catalog/growth-kit/sales-page-copy.md).

## When NOT to Change
- **Vanity Metrics:** Do not change the offer based on likes, reposts, or views without a DM or qualified comment.
- **Generic Praise:** "This looks cool" is not a signal. Wait for "How does this work for [My Specific Use Case]?"
- **Single Outliers:** Do not rewrite the offer for a buyer who is clearly outside the ICP (e.g., a "magic button" seeker).
- **Hypothesis only:** Never upgrade a "Hypothesis" to a "Verified" claim in the Sales Page without evidence from the [Proof Capture Log](./proof-capture-log.md).

## Friday Review Routine
*This routine should be performed every Friday as part of the [Weekly Offer Review](./weekly-offer-review.md).*

1. **Audit the Tracker:** Review the [Beta Conversation Tracker](./growth-audit-beta-conversation-tracker.md) and [Objection Bank](./growth-audit-objection-bank.md).
2. **Identify Patterns:** Look for repeating buyer phrasing or objections from the week.
3. **Log Decisions:** Fill out the Change-Log table above for any decided pivots.
4. **Deploy Changes:** Update the relevant Markdown files (Audit, Sales Page, or Storefront).
5. **Set Next Week's Test:** Define what "Hypothesis" we are testing based on this week's signal.
