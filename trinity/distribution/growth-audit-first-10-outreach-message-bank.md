# Growth Audit: First-10 Outreach Message Bank

## Mission
This bank provides high-status, manually personalized message skeletons for the first 10 outreach targets of the AI Growth Audit. These are designed for one operator talking to another, avoiding generic pitch language, fake urgency, and "AI will automate everything" framing.

---

## Proof-Safe Guardrails
*Refer to these before any outreach.*

1. **No Outcome Guarantees:** Never promise leads, revenue, or specific ROI.
2. **Use Diagnostic Language:** The Audit is a "map," "diagnostic," "blueprint," or "roadmap."
3. **AI as Leverage:** Position AI as a way to scale founder *judgment* and *taste*, not as a replacement for their voice.
4. **Lab Framing:** "I'm hardening this in the lab," or "Looking for an operator's eye-test on the diagnostic output."

---

## 1. Core Response Variants

### A. The "No-Pitch" First-Touch (Curiosity Wedge)
*Goal: Start a conversation by observing a specific "Loop Leak."*

> "Hey [Name], saw your post on [Specific Topic]. The technical depth in [Artifact] was great, but I noticed [Specific Loop Leak Observation - e.g., you mention these wins in comments but they never make it to the main feed]. I'm hardening a diagnostic at Trinity to help founders turn that depth into a repeatable system. Not pitching anything, just curious if that 'manual lifting' feeling is a bottleneck for you right now?"

### B. The Sample-Deliverable Follow-up
*Goal: Provide immediate value and build authority without a call.*

> "Appreciate the reply, [Name]. Since you're focused on [Specific Goal/Project], I thought you might value an eye-test on the **Growth Audit Sample Deliverable**. It shows exactly how we map a founder's distribution engine to find the primary leak. Happy to send the walkthrough over if you're curious to see the architectural spec."

### C. The Polite No-Fit / Not-Now Response
*Goal: Maintain status and protect lab time when there is a mismatch.*

> "Thanks for the context, [Name]. To be honest, based on [Specific Reason - e.g., you being in the early validation phase / your focus on automated lead-gen], I don't think the Growth Audit is the right fit for you just yet. We focus on scaling existing expertise and proof into a system. I'd recommend [Action from No-Fit Nurture Guide] for now. Once you have those first few artifacts, let's talk. Sound fair?"

## 2. The First-10 Message Skeletons

Each skeleton requires a manual **Research Note** before sending to ensure high-context alignment.

### 1. Technical Founder (Angle: The "Random Acts" Observation)
- **Research Note:** [Find a specific deep-tech post that had low engagement or no follow-up CTA.]

### 3. Subject Matter Expert (Angle: The "Ghost Town" Authority)
- **Research Note:** [Find an SME with deep expertise but very low posting frequency or stale profile.]

I'm testing a beta for an AI Growth Audit that extracts SME expertise into a distribution system. Mind if I send over a sample of the roadmap it produces?"

### 4. Subject Matter Expert (Angle: The "Trapped Expertise" Extraction)

## 3. CRM & Conversation Logging Fields
*Every interaction must be logged manually to feed the Offer Intelligence Loop. Reference the [Manual CRM Spec](./growth-audit-manual-crm-spec.md) for full definitions.*

### A. Target Account Log (Pre-Outreach)
*Log these in Tab 1 of the CRM for every prospect.*

- **Account/Name:** Identifier for the founder/company.
- **Segment:** (Tech Founder, SME, Operator, Anti-AI)
- **Fit Dimensions (0-2 each):** Expertise, Proof, Bottleneck, Alignment, Cadence.
- **Total Fit Score:** (0-10) Sum of dimensions.
- **First-Touch Angle:** The specific observation or wedge used from Section 2.
- **Current Stage:** (Target -> Researching -> Outreach Sent)

### B. Conversation Tracker Log (Post-Interaction)
*Log these in Tab 2 of the CRM for every reply or DM thread.*

- **Signal Score (1-5):** Quality of the interaction intent.
- **Exact Language:** Verbatim phrasing used by the buyer for bottlenecks or desires.
- **Objection:** Primary blocker (from the [Objection Bank](./growth-audit-objection-bank.md)).
- **Value Signal:** Which part of the audit resonated (POV, Proof, Loops, Roadmap).
- **Price Hypothesis:** Which pricing tier was tested (Wedge, Operator, Architect).
- **Price Reaction:** Verbatim language regarding cost.
- **Price Friction (1-5):** Intensity of price resistance.
- **Anchor/Alternative:** What they compared the Audit to (e.g., Consultant, Course, DIY).
- **Decision:** (Yes, Maybe, No, Nurture)
- **Confidence:** (Verified, Observed, Anecdotal, Hypothesis)
