# Trinity Distribution: Week 1 Warm Outbound & DM Pack

**Sprint:** Growth Distribution Sprint v1
**Owner:** Daniel Viveiros
**Objective:** Transition from public POV/Proof to direct, qualified conversations about the Distribution OS.
**Constraint:** No fake urgency, no fake outcomes, no commodity automation.

---

## 1. Target Segments
Focus on manual outreach to individuals where Daniel has existing context or where their public activity signals a specific "Distribution OS" bottleneck.

- **The Technical Solo-Founder:** Has a live product, visible technical depth, but "Random Acts of Content" syndrome. They often post sporadically or only about features.
- **The Subject Matter Expert (SME):** Deep expertise (e.g., in Data, PE, or Ops) but their LinkedIn is a "Ghost Town" or a "Press Release" feed.
- **The High-Leverage Operator:** Responsible for growth in a small team/portfolio but drowning in the manual friction of content creation and distribution.
- **The "Anti-AI" Professional:** Vocal about the flood of AI slop. They value taste and judgment and are skeptical of "automated growth."

---

## 2. Warm DM Openers (Distribution OS Focused)
These are for people Daniel is already connected with or has engaged with recently.

**Option 1: The "Random Acts" Observation**
> "Hey [Name], saw your post on [Topic]—really solid technical depth. I’ve been mapping out a 'Distribution OS' at my lab (Trinity) to help founders move away from 'Random Acts of Content' and into a more systematic engine. Curious, do you feel like you have a repeatable system for your POV yet, or is it still mostly manual lifting?"

> "[Name], I've been seeing a lot of 'AI content' slop lately and it's making me double down on the idea that AI should scale founder *judgment*, not replace it. I’m building a 'Growth Kit' around this right now. Would love to get your operator's take on whether 'scaling taste' is even on your radar this quarter?"

**Option 3: The "Proof vs. Guru" Angle**

I'm codifying a 'Proof Loop' as part of a Distribution OS—basically turning those wins into a repeatable engine. If you're open to it, I'd love to show you the visual spec I’m working on."

**Option 4: The Bottleneck Inquiry**
> "Hey [Name], I've been thinking about the four loops of distribution (POV, Proof, Conversations, Offers). I’ve noticed a lot of founders have the Proof but lack the Conversation loop. Where do you feel the biggest leak is in your own engine right now?"

**Option 5: The Lab Note Follow-up (Direct)**
> "[Name], just published a lab note on the 'Distribution OS' framework. It’s a bit of an 'anti-automation' take on founder growth. Given your focus on [Their Focus], I thought the 'POV Loop' section might resonate. Happy to send over the link if you're curious."

## 5. Red-Flag List (Do NOT Pitch)
Avoid these profiles to preserve Trinity’s premium/operator brand.

- **The "AI Magic" Seeker:** Anyone asking for "fully automated revenue" or "1,000 leads tomorrow."
- **The Zero-Proof Founder:** People with no product, no past results, and no clear expertise. Trinity scales proof; it cannot invent it.
- **The Engagement-Podder:** People who use generic "Great post!" comments or participate in vanity engagement loops.
- **The "Prompt Engineer" Enthusiast:** People obsessed with the latest "magic prompts" rather than business systems and workflows.
- **The Scale-At-All-Costs Cold-Outbounder:** Those looking for high-volume, low-quality scraping tools.

---

## 6. Proof Discipline Notes
*Apply these to every message you adapt from this pack.*

- **Avoid Fake Urgency:** Don't say "Only 2 spots left" or "Offer ends Friday."
- **Avoid Fake Outcomes:** Don't promise "10x engagement" or "Guaranteed pipeline."
- **Use Qualified Language:** Use "designed to identify," "aims to compress," "experimental framework," or "observed pattern."
- **Honor the Lab Status:** Frame Trinity as a lab where you are building and testing, not a "world-class agency" with hundreds of clients.
- **Focus on Judgment:** Always emphasize that the system is built to scale the *founder's* taste and expertise, not to replace it with a model's generic output.
