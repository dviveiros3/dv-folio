## 1. POV & Thesis Diagnosis
**Current State:** "We help B2B companies scale with AI." (Generic)
**Audit Finding:** The founder has deep experience in **mid-market supply chain logistics**, but the public-facing content is too broad. The unique "Point of View" (POV)—that AI in logistics should focus on *exception handling* rather than *full automation*—is buried in private sales calls.

**Proposed Pivot:**
- Move from "AI for B2B" to "Applied AI for Supply Chain Resilience."
- Anchor all content on the thesis: "The next 5 years of logistics efficiency won't come from better algorithms, but from AI-assisted human operators managing edge cases."

## 2. Proof Asset Inventory
**Available Proof:**
- 3 case studies on 15% reduction in "deadhead" miles (Provided by buyer; source verification required).

**Gap:** No "public" artifacts showing the *how*.
**Recommendation:** Create a "Workflow Teardown" exhibit in the Trinity Lab showing the exact logic used to identify high-cost exceptions.

## 3. Conversation Channels
**Current Primary Channel:** LinkedIn (Founder Profile).
**Assessment:** High engagement on "thought leadership" posts, but low conversion to DMs.
**Strategy:**
- Introduce "Signal-Led Outreach": Use AI to scan for logistics disruptions (e.g., port strikes, weather) and trigger personalized, POV-driven messages.
- **Planning Assumption:** This system is designed to facilitate a target of 5-10 qualified outbound-initiated conversations per week, up from a baseline of 2 random inbound inquiries per month.
