# AI Growth Audit: Buyer Intake Checklist

To ensure a high-conviction diagnostic and a 90-day roadmap that actually moves the needle, the founder must provide the following inputs before the 1-week audit begins.

## 1. Founder Context (The Voice)
- [ ] **Founder Briefing (15 min):** A recorded voice note or short video covering:
    - Your core market thesis (what you believe that others don't).
    - The specific problem you solve better than anyone else.
    - Your "dream buyer" profile.
- [ ] **"Gold Standard" Assets:** 3–5 examples of content, emails, or memos that perfectly capture your voice and expertise.

## 2. Proof Assets (The Evidence)
- [ ] **Outcome Data:** 2–3 examples of specific results you’ve delivered for clients (anonymized or simulated for the audit).
- [ ] **Methodology Docs:** Any internal frameworks, diagrams, or "how-to" guides you use to deliver your service.
- [ ] **Testimonials/Case Studies:** Existing proof of market validation.

## 3. Market Signals (The Targets)
- [ ] **Current ICP:** Who are you currently targeting, and why?
- [ ] **The "No" List:** A list of common objections or reasons why prospects haven't converted recently.
- [ ] **Signal Triggers:** What events or changes in a prospect's company make them a perfect fit right now? (e.g., new funding, hiring, specific tech usage).

## 4. Tech Stack & Channels (The Infrastructure)
- [ ] **Active Channels:** Which platforms are you currently using? (e.g., LinkedIn, Twitter, Substack).
- [ ] **Tooling:** Access or overview of your current CRM, outbound tools, and AI subscriptions (e.g., Claude, ChatGPT).
- [ ] **Current Cadence:** How often are you currently publishing or reaching out?

---

### Why we need this
The Trinity Distribution OS is not about generic automation. It is about **extracting your unique expertise** and scaling it. Without these inputs, the audit will produce generic results. With them, we can build an engine that sounds like you and sells for you.
