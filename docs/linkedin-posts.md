## Post #3: Educational (Post Wednesday 8am)

**Unpopular opinion:** Your ROAS is lying to you.

Here's why:

ROAS only looks at last-click attribution.

**Example:**
- Customer sees your LinkedIn ad (awareness)
- Clicks Facebook ad 3 days later (consideration)
- Converts on Google ad 2 days after that (conversion)

**Last-click says:** Google gets 100% credit
**Reality:** LinkedIn and FB drove the conversion too

**What this means:**
- You're probably over-investing in Google
- Under-investing in upper-funnel channels
- Making 6-figure budget decisions on incomplete data

**The fix:**
Multi-touch attribution that credits each touchpoint.

When you do this, you often find:
- LinkedIn drives 3x more value than last-click shows
- Facebook's real ROAS is 2x what you thought
- Google's role is smaller (but still important)

**Result?** Better budget allocation = lower CAC.

**Question for you:** Do you know your true CAC by channel, or just last-click?
